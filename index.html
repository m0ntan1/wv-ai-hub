7 innovation stories
One year post-HB 2014: transparency and grid-cost concerns surface. Ratepayer advocates and Appalachian Power warn that the law's microgrid backup provisions could raise customer bills, while environmental groups flag gaps in air-quality oversight for fast-tracked sites.Source: WV Gazette-Mail, Apr 2026 →
Residents call for protections as data-center buildout accelerates. Host communities in Berkeley, Tucker, and Mason counties are organizing for health, noise, and grid-impact protections, citing "empty promises" on community benefit and accountability.Source: West Virginia Watch, Mar 2026 →

Microgrid revenue reinvestment. Data center tax revenue flows 50% to income tax reduction, 30% to host county, 10% per-capita statewide, and 5% each to grid stabilization and economic grants.Source: Governor's Office, 2025 →
